Stretching

Overview:

  • Roll Calves - 30s
  • Static Calf Stretch - 30-60s
  • Roll Front of Hips - 30s
  • Roll Lats - 30s
  • Floor Lat Stretching - 1m
  • Roll Upper Back - 30s
  • Ball Roll Pectorals - 30s
  • Pectoral Stretching - 1m

Equipment

  • Foam Roller
  • Ball

Foam Roll Calves

30 sec each leg

Place a foam roller on the ground in front of you while sitting with your legs stretched out in front of you. Place one leg on top of the foam roller so that it is digging into your calf muscle. Place your other leg on top of your leg that is being rolled, and place your hands on the ground. Lift your butt off the oor using your upper body and roll back and forth on the calf.

Static Calf Stretch

30-60 sec each side

Put a foam roller on the ground, hold onto a wall or something else stable to help you balance, and put your toes up on the roller. Take the leg that you are not stretching and plant it on the oor in front of the roller. You will feel a deep stretching of the calf. To make this more effective you can activate the calf for 5 seconds by pressing the toes into the roller, and then relaxing the muscles to sink into a deeper stretch.

Foam Roll Front of Hips

30 sec each hip

Place a foam roller on the ground in front of you and orient your body on top of the foam roller so that the frontal mus- cles of one hip are on the foam roller. You should be leaning all of your body into the roller when doing this. Stay on the tissues, not the bone, and seek out “knots” or areas that feel tighter than other areas. If you nd these focus on them with your roller.

Foam Roll Lats

1x30 seconds on each side

Start seated and place a foam roller on the ground by your side. Outstretch your arm, lean over, and place your lat on the foam roller (just under the armpit). Roll back and forth on the lat making sure to reorient your body subtly so you can get the lat bers on your back, not just on your sides. Be VERY careful not to go too low with the roller as you can easily damage your lower ribs. When in doubt staying too high is preferable.

Floor Based Lat Stretching

1x1 minute on each side

Start on all fours in a curled position (similar to child’s pose from yoga). Take an arm and reach overhead and across the body with it and then plant the palm rmly on the oor. Keeping your body low to the ground, let your hips shift away from the planted palm in order to feel a stretch in the lats. Play around with different angles to produce the maximal amount of stretch on this muscle.

Foam Roll Upper Back

Sets 1x30 seconds

Start seated with a foam roller on the ground behind you. Cross your arms over your chest leaving your feet planted on the oor and lean back so that the roller is on your upper back. Lift your butt of the ground, keep the core very tight, and roll up and down the upper back.

Ball Release of Pectorals

1x30 seconds each side

Place your super nova (or other type of ball) on the ground. Put one pectoral directly onto the ball and place that arm behind the low back. With the arm behind the low back orient your body so that much of your body weight is on the ball. Roll around in this area and look for tight spots. If you are a woman you can still do this as your pectorals are above your breast tissue.

Pectoral Stretching

1x1 minute each side

Place your arm up on a doorway, wall, or machine about a vertical foot over the height of your shoulder. Keep the arm bone plugged backwards at the back end of the shoulder socket as you lean forward and allow your pec muscle to open up. Squeeze the shoulder blade of the shoulder that you are stretching towards the other shoulder blade and play around with the angles of this stretch until you can feel it deep in the pecs. If you are feeling this in the front of the shoulder instead of the pectorals then you are probably allowing your arm bone to drift forward, so make sure to keep it plugged back into the end of the socket

Mobility

Multidirectional Ankle Mobilization

1x15 reps each direction (inwards, straight forward, and outwards)

Place your toes facing toward a wall with about a st’s width between your toes and the wall. Ensure that the ankle stays planted rmly on the oor as your drive your knee forward toward the wall. The goal here is to repeatedly touch the wall with the knee, without letting the heel lift off the ground. You are going to perform 45 reps in total. 15 reps will be at an inward angle, 15 will be straightforward, and 15 will be off to the side.

Contract and Relax Anterior Hip Mobilization

2x15 each hip

Start in a kneeling position with one knee directly under your hip and the foot of the other leg planted on the oor in front of you. You want a 90 degree bend in both ankles. Squeeze your core and tilt your pelvis backwards as you rock/shift your hips forward squeezing the glute on the knee that is down, as hard as you can. You will shift forward and then come back and then shift forward a little bit deeper. This is done several times in sequence.

Thoracic Spine Mobilization

1x50 reps moving up and down the thoracic spine

Start laying on the oor with a foam roller placed at the same level as the top of your shoulder blades (just below the neck). Slightly tuck the pelvis, keep the knees bent with feet planted on the oor. Gently allow your body to bend over the foam roller and then crunch back up to a neutral position. After a couple of reps (5-10) you will move the foam roller down an inch or two. You will gradually work your way down to the lower thoracic spine where you will stop. Don’t do this on the lower back!

Shoulder Distraction

1 x 1 min each shoulder

Wrap a heavy duty resistance band around a stable, supporting structure. Wrap one end right around the head the the arm bone. Try to avoid the collar bone here, we are shooting straight for the top of the arm bone. Place the arm that the band is wrapped around behind the back and lean forward hard allowing the band to pull your shoulder back. When you get here just relax and prepare to hold the stretch for a minute.

Warmup

Wrist Rolls

15 x

Lace your fingers and roll your wrists back and forth.

Wrist Waves

15 x

Lace your ngers, open your palms, and make a wave motion with your hands. Make sure to switch the di- rection of the waves before stopping.

Deltoid Warm Up x all the way up and down

While standing, place your hands by your hips, ex your wrists, and start making a tight circular motion with your arms. Continue to make these small, tight circles as you take your arms all the way overhead and back down to the hips. Be sure to keep your stomach braced like you’re anticipating a punch here.

Arm Rotations

15 x

Hold your arms out to the side and rotate them back and forth to activate the rotator cuff muscles.

Arm Swings

10 x

Swing your arms across your chest a few times, alternating which arm is on top.

Seal Claps

15 x

Stand with your feet together with your arms outstretched and touching in front of you. Open your legs and arms simultaneously and then close them. Repeat the process over and over again similar to jumping jacks.

Body Weight Squats

15 x

Start standing with your feet shoulder width apart toes pointing forward. Hinge from the hips and lower your butt down towards the ground, keeping your chest as high as possible, and then return to standing.

Old Man Dance

x 15 clockwise x 15 counter clockwise

Place your feet and knees together, place your hands on your knees, and make clockwise and counterclock- wise circles with your knees.

Push Ups

10 x

Lace your fingers and roll your wrists back and forth.

Hip Bridge

10 x

Lay on your back with your knees bent, feet planted on the oor. Keep the core braced as you drive your heels into the ground sending your hips upwards into the air. Squeeze the glutes hard at the top.

Knee Roll Ups

10 x

Lie on your back with your knees bent, feet planted on the oor. Press your hands into the ground by your hips as you exhale and pull the knees into the chest.

Plank

30sec

Start on all fours. Place your elbows down on the oor, extend your feet into pushup position, hold this posi- tion isometrically squeezing the abs and butt as hard as you can. Make sure the hips are level with the shoulder and there is no sagging.

Phase 1 Day 1
Integrated Push Day

Equipment

  • Resistance Bands
  • Dumbbells
  • Bench
  • Cable Dings

Banded Pull Aparts

2x15 reps w/ 30 seconds rest in between sets

Stand with your feet shoulder width apart, lift the chest toward the sky, squeeze the stomach and butt, and make sure there is a slight bend in your knees. Hold your resistance band out in front of your chest and pull your arms out wide, as though you are giving someone a big hug. Focus on pushing your nger tips away from each other and squeeze the shoulder blades together as you perform this movement.

Single Arm Dumbbell Bench Press vs. Interset Pectoral Stretching

6x10 reps vs. 6x30 seconds each side

Start seated on a stable gym bench with your hands gripped around one dumbbell that is resting vertically on your lap. Keep both hands on the dumbbell handle as you lean back and lay on your back. As you lay back transition the dumbbell with you so that it is over your chest and carefully hand it off so that its only in one of your hands. Press your feet into the oor hard, keep your core and butt tight, and lower the dumbbell down towards the outside of your chest and then exhale hard and press it back up to the starting position focus on squeezing the pectoral and front deltoid muscle (chest and shoulder).

Place your arm up on a doorway, wall, or machine about a vertical foot over the height of your shoulder. Keep the arm bone plugged backwards at the back end of the shoulder socket as you lean forward and allow your pec muscle to open up. Squeeze the shoulder blade of the shoulder that you are stretching towards the other shoulder blade and play around with the angles of this stretch until you can feel it deep in the pecs. If you are feeling this in the front of the shoulder instead of the pectorals then you are probably allowing your arm bone to drift forward, so make sure to keep it plugged back into the end of the socket.

Single Arm Cable Overhead Press

6x10 reps each side w/ 45 sec – 1 min in between sets

Place a single handle attachment on a cable machine and place the cable pulley all the way down the ma- chine, as low as it goes. Stand adjacent to the pulley in an athletic stance (knees slightly bent, core tight, butt tight, chest lifted). Make sure that the pulley is directly underneath the shoulder that you are going to be training. Keep the core very tight, neck straight, and exhale to press the handle straight overhead. Once you complete one side you will immediately go into the next side. The set is over once the movement has been performed on both arms.

Seated Dumbbell Lateral Raises in Scapular Plane

6x10 reps w/ 45 sec – 1 min in between sets

Start seated upright with a bench that has back support. Start by holding the handle of two dumbbells that are resting on top of your knees. Keep the core braced, shoulders rolled back, and head on top of the spine (it’s easy to push it forward which we are NOT doing here). Raise the arms out away from the body at a 45 degree angle, lower them back down to the hips, and repeat. Go light here are rst. Although we are working in the scapular plane of motion (45 degrees away from the body) which tends to be easier on the shoulders, still go light. There will be plenty of time to build up enormous strength in this area as we progress through the program.

Isometric Push Up Hold

5x30 seconds during the set w/ 30 seconds rest in between sets

Assume a normal pushup position (hands directly under the shoulders with legs straight). Hold this position with a neutral spine while squeezing your whole body very tight. The most important part with this is the neutral spine. Keep the back very straight. Don’t let the hips sag or do anything weird or asymmetrical. Additionally, don’t let your head hang down towards the oor. This is an opportunity to improve forward head posture, so while you are doing this make sure to tuck your chin and push your neck back so that your neck spine (cervical) is very straight.

High Rep Face Pulls

2x50 reps w/ 1 min of rest in between sets

Wrap your resistance band or voodoo band around a column, machine, or something else that’s very stable and about the same height as your eye line. Back away from the supporting structure, take an athletic stance, exhale and pull the band toward the sides of your face. Focus on squeezing the shoulder blades together when doing this. We are going re- ally high rep here so prepare for a serious burn and pump in shoulder shoulders. Grind through it though and push yourself!

Traditional Cable Wood Chop

5x15 reps each side w/ 30 seconds of rest in between sets

Place a single handle attachment onto a cable machine and set the pulley so that it’s about a foot taller than your head. Stand adjacent to the pulley so that it’s exactly off to the side. Take an athletic stance that’s slightly wider than normal and grab the handle attachment with the hand closest to the cable handle and place your other hand on top of the hand that rst grabbed the handle (this step is important for optimal muscle recruitment). Keep the chest lifted high as you can and use your oblique muscles to pull the cable across your body toward the opposing hip while keeping the arms locked out and straight.

Elliptical HIIT Cardio

6x30 seconds intervals w/ 1-3 min of sustained resting speed in between intervals

Start out on an elliptical or stationary bike and a pace that is easy for you to sustain. When you are ready, adjust the resistance on the machine and set it to maximum. Go as hard, fast, and intense as humanly possible for 30 seconds and then place the resistance back down to easy and sustain this easy pace for 1-3 minutes (or until you feel recovered) and then repeat the process.

Phase 1 Day 2
Integrated Pull Day

Equipment

  • Voodoo Bands
  • Dumbbells
  • Bench
  • Cable Dings

Quadruped Flow

3x15 / 30 sec rest

Start on your hands and knees with your knees right under your hips and your hands right under your shoulders. Make sure your spine is neutral (straight). Suck the stomach in hard and keep it sucked in throughout the exercise. Additionally, tuck the chin toward the chest and press your neck back. Allow your shoulder blades to relax and pinch together and then press through your arms really hard into the oor sending your shoulder blades away from each other (protraction).

Arm Circles

2x15 reps / 30 sec rest

Wrap your voodoo band or resistance band around a stable column, machine, or anything that’s stable. Wrap at the same level as your eyes, and take an athletic stance. With the chest lifted and butt squeezed tight start by pull- ing your arms into your sides. Keep your arms as close to sideways as possible as your raise them up overhead. Once overhead, you can relax a little bit and allow the arms to move toward the cable before pulling them away from the cable again and lowering them back down to your sides the same way that you went up with them.

Single Arm Dumbbell Row vs. Interset Lat Stretching

6x10 reps / 1 min rest vs. 6x30 sec

Place the leg that’s on the same side as the arm your are performing this exercise on back, so it’s out of the way, and place the other foot closer to the bench, which you will be holding onto for stability. Hinge from the hips and place the hand of the arm you are not rowing with on a bench. Keep the spine neutral and try to stick your chest out as your per- form this. Exhale and pull the dumbbell up so that it is at the same levels as your chest, but just slightly off to the side.

Kneel down on both knees in front of your bench, pick a side to start with and extend that arm up and over to grab the bench over the opposing shoulder. Rock your hips back and shift your weight away from the hand grabbing the bench in order to place the lats under stretch. Play around with the nooks and crannies and nd the angles that stretch your lats the most. 30 seconds on each side makes this your entire rest time in between sets of rows.

Single Arm Lat Pull Downs vs. Interset Lat Stretching

6x10 reps each arm / 1 min rest vs. 6x30 sec

Start seated on a machine in which you can perform a traditional lat pull down, however take off any attach- ments that are on the cable and put a single handle attachment on there. Keep the chest lifted, put the arm that’s not working on the hip, and pull the arm in the air down towards the shoulder really squeezing the lat. It will look like you are a train conductor here. It’s a great unilateral movement for the lats.

Kneel down on both knees in front of your bench, pick a side to start with and extend that arm up and over to grab the bench over the opposing shoulder. Rock your hips back and shift your weight away from the hand grabbing the bench in order to place the lats under stretch. Play around with the nooks and crannies and nd the angles that stretch your lats the most. 30 seconds on each side makes this your entire rest time in between sets of rows.

Farmer’s Walk

5x30 seconds / 1 min rest

Start standing with two heavy dumbbells on the oor right by your feet. Squat down to pick up the dumb- bells keeping your spine neutral and stand up with them. Maintain PERFECT posture (chest open and head on straight, not pushed forward) as you begin to walk while simply holding the dumbbells. This is an excellent exercise the will really tax your grip strength and shoulder girdle.

Reverse Cable Wood Chops

5x15 reps each side / 30 sec rest

Place a single handle attachment onto a cable machine and set the pulley so that it’s at the bottom, as low as it can go. Stand adjacent to the pulley so that it’s exactly off to the side. Take an athletic stance that’s slightly wider than normal and grab the handle attachment with the hand closest to the cable handle and place your other hand on top (this step is important for optimal muscle recruitment). Keep the chest lifted high as you use your oblique muscles to pull the cable across your body, up, and towards the opposing shoulder.

Plank

5x30 seconds / 30 sec rest

Extend your legs back so they are in push up position. Instead of placing your hands on the oor as though were were going to perform a push up, place the elbows on the ground and lift your body up. Only your forearms and toes should contact the ground. Squeeze the core muscles very hard as you nish your workout with this exercise. Make sure your spine is straight when performing this. Having a mirror beside you can help tremendously, otherwise you can have someone record you while you perform it to make sure your spine is neutral.

Phase 1 Day 3
Integrated Lower Body Day

Equipment

  • Plastic Pipe
  • Gym Ball
  • Dumbells
  • Cable Dings

Hip Hinge Movement Pattern Re-programming

100 reps

Stand in an athletic stance with your feet shoulder width apart, slight bend in your knees, and chest lifted high. Place a stick, painting rod, dowel rod, or broomstick on your back. Ensure that the stick is in contact with the back of the head, the upper back, and the back of the pelvis at all times. From here, bend forward by hinging at the hips (like a door hinge) and then return back to standing. Make sure the stick stays in contact with the three areas discussed previ- ously. In essence, this awesome movement pattern reprogramming exercise that will teach your body how to move cor- rectly, keep a neutral spine, and protect your back! You can do this before you even hit the gym. If you do though, make sure you do your dynamic warm up once you arrive at the gym.

Standing Leg Abductions

4x15 each leg / 30s rest

Stand next to a machine or column of which you can hold onto for support and stability. Stay upright with your body as your raise one leg out to the side making sure the toes remain pointing straight forward and return that leg back to the ground.

Counter Balanced Single Leg Deadlifts

5x10 reps each / 45s rest

Hold a dumbbell in one hand and start in an athletic stance (you should know what that is by now). Gently lift the leg that’s on the sames side of the arm in which you are holding the dumbbell. At this point you should be standing on one leg, make sure that leg has a slight bend in it as you hinge from the hips and lower your body towards the ground. Once your hip angle is at approximately 90 degrees then return back to standing by squeezing the glutes on the leg you are balancing on.
For this exercise it’s VERY important that you keep your knee on the standing leg bent slightly until the very top of the movement. Secondly, it’s crucially important that you keep your hips squared and facing towards the oor with this exercise. Do not allow your hips to open and point off to the side. Keeping the hip oriented towards the ground will help you achieve more muscular balance and optimal muscle recruitment patterns.

Bulgarian Split Squats

5x10 reps each / 1m rest

Hold a dumbbell in each hand and place one foot on top of a bench or box. Make sure that the other foot is planted rmly on the ground and is far enough away from the bench so that when you bend it to 90 degrees the knee re- mains over the ankle. To perform the movement, all you are doing is bending both knees by lowering yourself towards the ground until your knees reach approximately 90 degrees and then you will return back to the starting position.
It’s very easy to push the leg that’s resting on the bench into the bench to assisst with this movement. This is cheating though so try to avoid that. Keep the leg on the bench as relaxed as possible, and force the leg that’s on the ground to do all the work.

Dumbbell Walking Lunges

5x10 each / 1m rest

Stand with with a dumbbell in each hand. Step forward with one leg and bend both knees to approximately 90 degrees and then step the back foot forward to meet the other foot. Alternate sides as you perform this. You are per- forming 10 reps (steps forward) with each leg so 1x10 each leg perform in one set is actually 20 reps in total (10 on the right, 10 on the left).

High Rep Body Weight Squats vs. Chair Pose

4x20 vs. 4x30s

Start with your feet shoulder width apart, toes pointing forward, in an athletic stance. Lower your body down by hinging from the hips and bending the knees while striving to keep your chest upright and heels planted on the ground. Exhale and return to standing by squeezing the legs.

As soon as you nish your BW squats stay in your squat stance, extend your arms overhead, and lower your- self down into the bottom of the squat position and hold. Hold this for 30 seconds. It’s gonna burn real bad so you need to be tough here!
Really lift that chest high here and never let it sink lower than the hips. You should try to remain as vertical with your upper body as possible with this exercise.

Stability Ball Crunches

5x20 / 30s rest

Start with your back on a stability ball. The ball should be in between your hips and shoulder blades on your back. Lace your ngers behind your neck to support it, but make sure you are not pulling on your neck. Allow your abdominals to relax which will allow your spine to bend and extend over the ball. Once at the bottom of the movement, squeeze the abs and pull yourself up just a little bit beyond straight before returning back to the start of the movement.
This is a great exercise for the abs. Make sure when you are doing this that the room is clear of sharp or hard de- bris in case you roll over it with the ball or fall off.

Palloff Press

5x15 each / 30s rest

Stand adjacent to a cable machine and set the pulley so that it’s level to your breast bone. Come off to the side of the machine, wrap your ngers around the handle attachment, and take an athletic stance. Exhale hard as you push your arms in front of you in a straight line and then return them back towards the chest. Do not allow the cable to pull you out of position. Your job here is to ght the rotation and keep everything pointed straight forward.
This is a great anti-rotation movement for the core. It teaches your body to resist rotational forces that act on the lumbar spine which really helps to stabilize this area and prevent injury.
